01Ninja + Alchemist
Ninja Monkey 5/0/2 + Alchemist 4/2/0

A crosspathed Grandmaster Ninja relies on external buffs to be worth its cost. Stand a 4/2/0 Alchemist next to it for Berserker Brew, and the buffed Ninja becomes real DPS instead of cost-inefficient on its own. (A separate 0/4/0 Ninja is usually added on top of this for DDT insurance — it's a different tower doing a different job.)

02Village + Super Monkey
Monkey Village 0/2/3 + Super Monkey 5/2/0

0/2/3 Village gives camo detection and lets towers pop any bloon type. Park a 5/2/0 True Sun God inside its radius for max range and damage output.

03Alchemist buff-chain
Alchemist 4/2/0 → Alchemist 4/2/0

Place multiple 4/2/0 Alchemists in range of each other so each one's Berserker Brew buffs the next, compounding attack speed and damage.

04Village-stacking
Village 4/2/0 + Village 0/2/3

Overlap a 4/2/0 Village (Primary buffs + camo) with a 0/2/3 Village (camo + pop-any-bloon) so your core towers get multiple buffs at once.

05Tack Zone core
Tack Shooter 2/0/5 + Alchemist 4/2/0 + Village 0/2/3

2/0/5 The Tack Zone buffed by both Alchemist and Village becomes a budget late-game wall of DPS.

06Glue + damage dealer
Glue Gunner 0/2/3 + Bomb Shooter 2/5/0

0/2/3 MOAB Glue locks MOAB-class bloons in place while a 2/5/0 MOAB Eliminator nukes them with its ability at leisure.

Round numbers are identical on every difficulty — the same bloons appear on the same round number regardless of Easy/Medium/Hard. The only differences are bloon speed, tower/upgrade costs, starting lives, and the round 40 MOAB having 66% HP on Easy specifically. What changes is how far you have to go: Easy ends at round 40, Medium at round 60, Hard at round 80, and Impoppable/CHIMPS both run to round 100.

If you're playing outside CHIMPS (standard, Impoppable with farming, or Freeplay) the full True Sun God becomes realistic once you can farm past that ~$180k ceiling — at that point a single 5/0/0 or 5/2/0 True Sun God really can become the centerpiece of the whole defense, sacrificing other towers into it rather than running them alongside it.

Only one hero per game, but place one almost always — even a weak hero levels up for free and eventually outpaces most towers. Picking the right one just changes how much it carries. Rankings below merge two sources that genuinely disagree in places — noted where that happens.

Heads up: one of the two sources ranks heroes specifically for round-100 CHIMPS runs. That skews some placements — Sauda and Benjamin in particular land lower than they'd realistically be for a shorter Easy/Medium run, since Sauda's penalty is a late-game nerf she never lives long enough to feel, and Benjamin's penalty (no income in CHIMPS) doesn't apply outside that mode. Likewise, Adora and Corvus lean on long leveling curves that pay off only if the game runs long enough to reach them.
