Round numbers are identical on every difficulty — the same bloons appear on the same round number regardless of Easy/Medium/Hard. The only differences are bloon speed, tower/upgrade costs, starting lives, and the round 40 MOAB having 66% HP on Easy specifically. What changes is how far you have to go: Easy ends at round 40, Medium at round 60, Hard at round 80, and Impoppable/CHIMPS both run to round 100.

Round 40 — Primary Only

This mode restricts you to the 6 Primary-category towers (Dart Monkey, Boomerang, Bomb Shooter, Tack Shooter, Ice Monkey, Glue Gunner) — no Magic, Military, or Support, so no Wizard, Alchemist, or Spike Factory. Round 60 — Medium

Medium's budget can't max every piece the way a full CHIMPS run could, so a few things are deliberately capped or skipped below. Only one hero per game, but place one almost always — even a weak hero levels up for free and eventually outpaces most towers. Tiers below come from the same BTD6 Tower Tier List doc as the Tier List tab (see Sources tab) — it ranks heroes alongside tower paths in one unified S–F scale. Dan D'Monke isn't covered by that doc; his entry is kept from earlier research.

S-

Brickell

Good earlygame that buffs Monkey Sub, one of the strongest earlygame towers; stacked Mega Mines have huge DPS potential but can trigger dangerous MOAB/ceramic rushes if overdone. Level 17 also gives her solid single-target pistol damage.

Corvus

Extremely powerful at every stage since he runs on mana, not your money — just keep him near bloons to keep mana flowing. High-level play can reach level 20 by round 95, but demands constantly casting spells; playing him passively is weaker but still viable.

A

Striker Jones

Fires too slowly to shine early, but his level 3 ability shuts down a lane of round 63 and wrecks 76/78; outside Bomb/Mortar strategies, his Concussive Shell and range/pierce buffs still add value.

Psi

Instantly kills most earlygame bloons and his level 3 stun clumps groups for easy cleanup; level 14/16 power spikes crush MOAB density lategame, but he deals no BAD damage so needs a T5 like MOAB Eliminator alongside him.

Rosalia

High-damage laser carries the earlygame without needing camo detection; her moab-knockback laser is the real lategame value for stalling, with Kinetic Charge as a strong round 98/100 finisher.

A-

Geraldo

Glue, pickles, and turrets make for a strong earlygame. Most of his kit is a band-aid for specific hard rounds rather than constant value, so don't overspend on items and let his Pet Bunny do the lategame attacking.

Obyn Greenfoot

Strong vs early rounds, but can't be afforded by round 6 and his main attack falls off quickly after. Totems' MOAB slowdown and synergy with top-path Druids (level 18 unlocks Total Transformation on Druids of Wrath) make him a strong Druid-strategy pick.

Etienne

Weak until his drones get a real boost at level 7/9; UCAV gives decent grouped DPS and a big range buff at level 16, but Perma-UCAV eats into end-of-round stall time other towers rely on.

Gwendolin

Cocktail covers the earlygame and doubles as midgame ceramic cleanup when alched; Heat It Up's consistent lead-popping and ramping burn damage on MOABs is the main reason to run her.

B

Quincy

Decent earlygame, but struggles vs dense regular bloons and can't damage DDTs at all; works as a supporting damager all game, most effective in the 90s paired with Glue Strike or Permanent Brew.

Captain Churchill

Affordable as early as round 24–30 with a strong earlygame; pair with a slow tower to clump targets for his low-attack-speed cannons. A lategame buff now lets him solo every ZOMG layer in the game.

Ezili

Weak earlygame, but very good MOAB DPS from level 6 on, and Hex can clear the insides of up to 24 MOABs when timed well. Her level 20 round-100 insurance is unusually cheap on expert maps.

Adora

High-pierce homing projectiles give a good earlygame, but she's intentionally weak midgame while you bank Blood Sacrifice fodder for her level 20 lategame spike — recent nerfs hurt that spike's killing power.

Silas

Infuriating but solid earlygame thanks to his attack's randomness; rushing tier-3 Ice Monkeys gets him to level 20 early, after which his rotating ice shards pair extremely well with MOAB freezers.

Dan D'Monke

Flexible dual-form support/DPS hero; solid but unspectacular, good Reposition utility. Not covered by the current tier doc — placement kept from earlier research.

B-

Sauda

Can leak against big single bloons like Purples/Zebras early. Her damage leans entirely on prey effects from stunned/slowed bloons (Relentless Glue activates it well), and she's generally outclassed by strategies favoring high range, which she lacks.

C

Pat Fusty

Nothing special early beyond strong round-40 knockback. His level 3 damage buff is excellent on low-damage/high-speed towers like Grandmaster Ninja or Tack Zone, but his short range hurts on maps with multiple separate lanes.

C-

Benjamin

Lower levels are worthless with income/life generation disabled. His level 10 ability can delete huge amounts of RBE on the hardest rounds (at the cost of losing the money from the downgrade), making him a niche pick for towers that struggle vs density, like Perma-Spike.
